Before You Apply

Eligibility Requirements

Review the basic requirements before starting your application. Most students at U.S. colleges and universities qualify.

ℹ️
Meeting these requirements does not guarantee aid — it means you are eligible to apply. Aid amounts depend on financial need and program availability.

📋 Basic Eligibility

  • Be a U.S. citizen or eligible non-citizen
  • Have a valid Social Security Number (SSN)
  • Have a high school diploma or GED
  • Be enrolled or accepted into an eligible degree program
  • Maintain satisfactory academic progress
  • Not be in default on a federal student loan

🏫 School Requirements

  • Enrolled at least half-time in most programs
  • School must be a Title IV-participating institution
  • Working toward a degree or certificate
  • Undergraduate or graduate student status
  • Not have exceeded loan limits for your program

📄 Financial Requirements

  • Demonstrate financial need (for need-based aid)
  • Provide tax return information (yours and/or parents')
  • Report all household income and assets
  • No requirement to have a minimum income
  • Independent students assessed separately

⚠️ Disqualifying Factors

  • Currently incarcerated in a federal/state facility
  • Defaulted on a prior federal student loan
  • Owe a refund on a previous federal grant
  • Convicted of a drug offense while receiving aid
  • Not enrolled in an eligible program
